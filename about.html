About Kenya Harlequin FC

Learn about our heritage, our home venue at Ngong Road, and the partners supporting us.

Our Rich Heritage

Kenya Harlequin Football Club is one of the most successful and decorated rugby clubs in East Africa. Our history is deeply linked to the development of the sport in the region.

1923

Early Beginnings

Following a split in the Nairobi District Team, the first iteration of the Harlequins rugby club was formed in Nairobi. They played matches against early colonial settlements and visiting naval ships.

1952

Official Affiliation

Led by Peter Barron and Ben Cook, the club was formally established and secured an official affiliation with Harlequin F.C. in London. They were granted permission to wear the iconic multi-colored quarters and badge.

1964

The Inaugural Christie 7s

The club hosted the first ever Christie 7s tournament, named in honor of Alex "Sandy" Milne Christie, a past President of Kenya Harlequins and RFUEA. Today, it is Africa's oldest 7s tournament.

2026

Modern Era

With 8 Kenya Cup trophies and countless local cups, Quins continues to build its legacy at the RFUEA Grounds. We are actively expanding women's programs and youth academies.

Our Home Grounds

Kenya Harlequins hosts all home matches at the RFUEA Grounds, located along Ngong Road in Nairobi. It is the spiritual home of Kenyan rugby.
